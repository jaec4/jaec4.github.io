Cube Lamp

1. Measure the Lamp

To create a cap which can go through the line and be fixed on the plastic, I measured a width of an outlet, width of a top part of the plastic, width of a mid part of the plastic, and the length between the top part and the mid part where a tangent starts.

2. Create a Cap on Rhino

3. Print a Cap and Try

I printed out to check whether the cap fits well into the plastic before I go further. I had to print out twice to make a perfect fit. After I printed out the first one, I adjusted little bit to make the second one fit better.


Tip: For cap to be fit well, you should make the cap size sligtly smaller than the original size. Because of the attribute of PLA, the cap will stretch little bit to to hold on to the plastic.

4. Create a Cube in Rhino

5. Convert STL to G-Code on Cura

The estimated completion time of the first one said 3 days 19 hours 25 minutes. The other one on the right said 2 days 13 hours 48 minutes. I did not see this coming, but since I am here, I decided to proceed with the one on the right.

6. Print

It took exactly 48 hours to complete. I learned that I should not do this next time. I was really worried that a little error messed up everything or PLA ran out in the middle of the long process. Second, because I worried this too much, I unintentionally woke up in the middle of the nights to check whether it was going allright. Next time, if the object needs huge walls (the supports), I would find different ways that does not require the walls.
